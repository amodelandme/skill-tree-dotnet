## Tone

- **Casual or formal?** Casual-technical — like explaining something to a smart colleague over coffee, not presenting at a conference
- **How much personality?** Enough to sound human. Dry wit is fine. Self-deprecating about mistakes is fine. Motivational is not.
- **Technical depth?** Name the technology, explain the concept briefly, don't over-explain. Assume the reader is a developer but not necessarily a .NET specialist.

---

## Things I never want in a post

- "I'm excited to share..."
- "On my journey..."
- "Humbled and grateful..."
- "Let's connect!"
- "Feel free to reach out!"
- Walls of text with no white space
- Vague claims without a specific example
- More than 5 hashtags

---

## Things I want more of

- Specific technology names — not "a library" but "FluentValidation v12"
- Honest about being wrong before being right
- The interesting technical decision, not just the outcome
- Questions at the end that invite real responses, not just likes
